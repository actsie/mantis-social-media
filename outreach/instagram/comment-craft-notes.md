# Instagram Comment Craft Notes — How We Comment

**Purpose:** Document the thinking/reasoning behind how we craft Instagram comments. This is NOT a template to copy — it's a reference for the craft.

---

## What We've Learned (From 50+ Comments)

### **The Core Pattern**

Good Instagram comments do two things:
1. **React to something specific** — name a detail, technique, color, shape
2. **Optionally ask one question** — about their process, client requests, or experience

---

## Examples Analyzed

### ✅ Good Comment (Specific + Question)
**Post:** @jjsthreadingsalon — before/after layer cut

**Our comment:**
> this before and after is so satisfying! the layers changed the whole look. do people usually come in knowing they want this or do you figure it out together?

**Why it works:**
- Validates the transformation ("so satisfying")
- Names what changed ("the layers")
- Asks about their process (not about us)
- Two options in the question (shows we understand both paths)

---

- Validates it worked ("somehow it all just works")
- No question needed — hype alone is complete

## Key Principles

### **1. Name Something Specific**
| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| "the taupe base plus the white swirls" | "love these!" |
| "the deep red one is genuinely so rich" | "so pretty!" |
| "the edge heart on a dark set" | "cool design" |

**Why:** Specificity shows you actually looked, not just reacting to the vibe.

---

### **2. Hype Alone Is Complete (Sometimes)**
| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| "that red is so clean. rounded square is doing a lot for it" | "gorgeous!! what polish is this? how long did it take? do you do bookings?" |
| "this is a whole moment" | "love this! do you have a website? can I book?" |

**Why:** Not every comment needs a question. Sometimes hype IS the comment.

---

### **3. Questions About THEIR Experience (Not Ours)**
| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| "do clients usually book her by name?" | "can I book with her?" |
| "do people come in knowing what they want?" | "do you take walk-ins?" |
| "is this your own design?" | "can you do this for me?" |

**Why:** We're building presence, not soliciting. Questions should be about their business/process.

---

### **4. Acknowledge the Risk/Choice**
| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| "every nail is doing something different and somehow it all just works" | "great set" |
| "was not on my radar today but now I need this" | "nice colors" |
| "the edge heart on a dark set" (unexpected combo) | "pretty nails" |

**Why:** Shows you understand what makes the design interesting, not just that it's pretty.

---

### **5. Use "Genuinely" Sparingly (Once Per Day Max)**
**Note:** "Genuinely" is not banned on IG (unlike X/Reddit), but overuse makes it feel templated.

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Use 1x per day max | Use in every comment |
| "the deep red one is genuinely so rich" | "this is genuinely so good" (generic) |

**Why:** Even good words lose impact when overused.

## Question Types That Work

### **Process Questions**
- "do clients usually get to pick the design or do you mostly just freestyle?"
- "do people come in knowing exactly what they want or do you figure it out together?"

**Why:** Shows interest in their craft, not just the result.

---

### **Technique Questions**
- "are those segment lines done with tape or foil?"
- "did you freehand all of these?"
- "how long does this set take to complete?"

**Why:** Shows we know enough to ask the right questions.

---

### **Business Questions**
- "do clients usually book her by name or is it more walk-in?"
- "do most clients add this on at booking or is it more of an in-session upgrade?"
- "do people book this more for events or is it a regular everyday request?"

**Why:** Subtly signals we think about the business side (our ICP).

---

## What We Don't Do

### ❌ Don't Use Generic Hype
**Bad:** "gorgeous!!" "so pretty!" "love your work!"
**Good:** "the taupe base plus the white swirls" "the deep red one is genuinely so rich"

**Why:** Generic = bot. Specific = human.

---

### ❌ Don't Ask About Us
**Bad:** "can I book?" "do you have a website?" "what's your pricing?"
**Good:** "do clients usually book by name?" "do people book this for events or everyday?"

**Why:** We're building presence, not soliciting.

---

### ❌ Don't List Every Element
**Bad:** "love the colors, the shape, the length, the design, everything is perfect!!"
**Good:** "the silver swirls over the brown glitter base is such a good call"

**Why:** Listing everything feels like a checklist. Pick ONE thing and go deep.

---

### ❌ Don't Template the Structure
**Bad:** Every comment is "[specific thing] + [question]"
**Good:** Mix it up — sometimes hype only, sometimes observation only, sometimes question

**Why:** Templates feel robotic. Variety feels human.

---

### ❌ Don't Use Em Dashes or Hyphens
**Bad:** "the deep red — so rich!"
**Good:** "the deep red is genuinely so rich"

**Why:** Dashes read as AI-casual. Periods are cleaner.

---

### ❌ Don't Use Quotation Marks
**Bad:** "the 'edge heart' design is cool"
**Good:** "the edge heart on a dark set"

**Why:** Quotes around words read as sarcastic or fake on Instagram.

---

## Banned Words/Phrases (Instagram)

- weird, resonate, nightmare, amazing, stunning, quiet, especially
- vibe/vibes (use "energy" or "feel" instead)
- plot twist, lands, sticks, clicks
- read/reads (as in "reads clearly" — say "shows clearly" instead)
- bingo card, frame/framing
- "you should", "you need to" (preachy)
- "love your content!" (generic bot energy)

**Note:** "Genuinely" and "actually" are OK sparingly on IG (unlike X/Reddit), but don't overuse.

---

## Tone Check Before Posting

Ask:
1. **Did I name something specific (color, shape, technique, detail)?**
2. **Am I asking about their process/experience (not about me booking)?**
3. **Is this hype-only, observation-only, or observation + question? (pick one)**
4. **Did I avoid listing every element? (just 1-2 specifics)**
5. **Any banned words?**
6. **No dashes or quotation marks?**

## Platform Differences

| Instagram | Reddit | X (Twitter) |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Hype + specific detail | Validate + add one thing | State insight + personal reflection |
| Questions about their process | Questions only if genuine | No questions (usually) |
| 1-3 sentences | 2-4 sentences | 1-3 sentences |
| Emojis OK (sparingly) | Emojis rare | Emojis rare |
| "Genuinely" OK sparingly | "Genuinely" banned | "Genuinely" banned |

---

## The Craft in One Sentence

**Name something specific they did → React to it genuinely → Optionally ask about their process (not about you).**

---

**This is craft documentation, not a template.** Use the reasoning, not the structure. Every comment should feel like a real person reacting, not a formula being applied.
