# Tweet Craft Notes — How We Build Posts

**Purpose:** Document the thinking/reasoning behind how we craft tweets. This is NOT a template to copy — it's a reference for the craft.

---

## Example: Evening Reminders Tweet (Mode 1 — Leads)

**Final tweet:**
> ran across a thread where a dental clinic cut no-shows in half by switching from calls to texts. evening reminders at 6-7pm get way better response rates than morning-of. people are on their phones in the evening and actually have time to think about tomorrow. morning reminders get buried in the rush. makes sense the more i think about it.

---

## How We Built This

### 1. **Source the insight from real conversations**
- Read actual Reddit threads where business owners share experiences
- Pull from top-voted comments (real people, real results)
- This one came from u/N0omi's comment about evening vs. morning timing

### 2. **Build context for X audience**
- Reddit commenters already know the thread context
- X audience doesn't — so we open with "ran across a thread where..."
- One sentence to set the scene, then get to the insight

### 3. **State the insight directly**
- No filler like "the insight that hit" or "the insight that stuck"
- Just: "evening reminders at 6-7pm get way better response rates than morning-of"
- Specific times (6-7pm) not vague ("evening")

### 4. **Add the "why" behind the insight**
- "people are on their phones in the evening and actually have time to think about tomorrow"
- This makes it feel observed, not prescriptive

### 5. **Acknowledge the flip side**
- "morning reminders get buried in the rush"
- Shows we understand both sides, not just preaching one answer

### 6. **End with personal reflection, not directive**
- ❌ Bad: "worth thinking about if you're sending at 9am" (implies they're doing it wrong)
- ❌ Bad: "you should switch to evening" (preachy)
- ✅ Good: "makes sense the more i think about it" (us processing, not telling)

---

## Key Principles

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| State insights directly | Use filler like "the insight that hit/stuck" |
| Build context for X (they don't know the Reddit thread) | Assume they know what you're talking about |
| End with personal reflection ("makes sense to me") | End with directives ("you should", "worth thinking about if") |
| Be specific (6-7pm, not "evening") | Be vague |
| Sound like you're processing out loud | Sound like you're teaching |
| Pull from real conversations | Make up stats or insights |

---

## Banned Words/Phrases (for tweets)

- weird, resonate, nightmare, amazing, stunning, quiet, especially
- vibe/vibes, genuinely, actually, plot twist
- lands, sticks, clicks, read/reads
- bingo card, frame/framing
- hit (as in "the insight that hit")
- stuck (as in "the insight that stuck")
- "worth thinking about if" (condescending)
- "you should" (preachy)

---

## Tone Check Before Posting

Ask:
1. Does this sound like me processing out loud, or me teaching?
2. Did I build enough context for someone who hasn't read the source thread?
3. Is the ending collaborative or directive?
4. Are there any banned words?
5. Is it specific enough to feel real?

---

**This is craft documentation, not a template.** Use the reasoning, not the structure. Every tweet should feel fresh, not templated.
